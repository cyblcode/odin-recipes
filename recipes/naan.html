Naan

Description

This homemade naan recipe makes a soft, chewy naan with a buttery taste. We would always order this at a Indian restaurant and it always tastes great, espcially when you dip it in a curry. Here is a recipe to make a naan as good as it is in restaurants!

Ingredients

  • 1 (.25 ounce) package active dry yeast
  • 1 cup warm water
  • ¼ cup white sugar
  • 3 tablespoons of milk
  • 1 large egg, beaten
  • 2 teaspoons of salt
  • 4 ½ cups of bread flour
  • 2 teaspoons of minced garlic (Optional)
  • ¼ cup butter, melted

Steps

  • Dissolve yeast in warm water in a large bowl. Let stand about 10 minutes, until frothy.
  • Meanwhile, generously oil a large bowl.
  • Stir sugar, milk, egg, and salt into the yeast mixture. Mix in enough flour to make a soft dough.
  • Knead dough on a lightly floured surface until smooth. (6-8 minutes)
  • Place dough in the prepared oil, cover with a damp cloth and let it rise until the dough is doubled in size. (About 1 hour)
  • Punch down dough on a lightly floured surface, and knead in garlic. Pinch off small handfuls of dough about the size of a golf ball; you should have about 14. Roll each piece into a ball and place on a tray. Cover with a towel, and allow it to rise, until it is doubled in size. (30 minutes)
  • Meanwhile, preheat a large grill pan over high heat.
  • Roll each piece into a thin circle.
  • Brush some melted butter on the pan and place as many pieces you can fit and cook unitl puffy and lightly browned. (2 to 3 minutes). Brush the butter on the uncooked sides, flip and cook (2-4 more minutes). Remove from grill and cook the remaining naan
  • Enjoy your naan!
