Makhani Chicken (Indian Butter Chicken)

Description

This delicious butter chicken recipe is from the owner of India Cuisine restuarant in Seattle, Washington. It takes 5 hours and 35 minutes to make because of the 4 hour marination process.
It serves up to 6 people and is best served with naan.

Ingredients

Marinade

  • 1¾ pounds skinless, boneless chicken breast halved (cubed)
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice, divided
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder, divided
  • Salt to taste*
  • 1 cup of yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons of olive oil
  • 2 tablespoons of ginger paste
  • 2 tablespoons of garlic paste
  • 2 tablespoons of melted butter
  • 1½ teaspoons of garam masala
* Adjust the salt according to your taste

Sauce

  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • 1 tablespoon of garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon of garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon of chopped garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of chopped green chili pepper
  • 2 cups of tomato puree
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 tablespoon of chili powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 1 tablespoon of honey
  • ½ teaspoon of dried fenugreek leaves
  • 1 cup of heavy cream

Steps

Making the marinade

  1. Place chicken in a nonporous glass dish or bowl and add 1 tablespoon of lemon juice, 1 tablespoon of chili powder, and salt; stir to evenly coat chicken.
  2. Cover the dish and let it marinate in the refrigerator for about 1 hour.
  3. Line a strainer with cheesecloth and place yogurt to allow it to drain for 15 to 20 minutes.
  4. Transfer drained yogurt to a medium bowl and mix the remaining 2 tablespoons of lemon juice, oil, garlic & ginger paste, melted butter, remaining 1 tablespoon of chili powder, and garam masala into yogurt until well combined.
  5. Pour the yogurt mixture over chicken, cover the dish, and refrigerate once more to marinate for about 3 to 4 hours.
  6. Preheat the oven to 400F (200C)
  7. Place chicken on skewers; arrange in a 9x13 inch baking dish
  8. Bake in the preheated oven until almost cooked through for about 20 minutes.

Making the sauce

  1. Melt butter in a medium saucepan over medium heat and stir in garam masala.
  2. When masala begins to crackle, mix in ginger paste, garlic, and green chile powder.
  3. Saute for 2 to 3 minutes until tender and stir in tomato puree, water, chili powder, and salt.
  4. Bring to a boil; reduce heat to low and simmer, stirring in honey and fenugreek.
  5. Remove par-cooked chicken from skewers and place into sauce in the pan. Continue cooking for about 5 minutes until chicken is no longer pink inside.
  6. An instant read thermometer placed in the center should read 165F (74C).
  7. Stir in cream until incorporated.
